# Pirola Line — Research Summary

## What We Know

### Luigi "Gino" Pirola (1905–2002) — Hardy's Great-Grandfather

Born 1905 in **Cernusco sul Naviglio**, near Milan, Italy. From a modest Catholic family with roots in **cheesemaking and local trade**. Worked as a textile company clerk, then became an international businessman.

**Life timeline:**
- 1905: Born in Cernusco sul Naviglio (Lombardy, Italy)
- ~1928–1930: Married **Grace Therese Bailor** (English) in France. They communicated first in French, then Spanish.
- ~1930: Son Antonio born (3 Apr 1930)
- ~1930s: Son Romano "Ron" born in **Mexico** while family was traveling for business
- 1939: Left Italy to avoid fascist conscription and protect English wife. Began extensive international business — Latin America, Western Europe, India, Pakistan, China, Korea, Eastern Europe
- 1948: "An Italian, Luigi Pirola" arrested in Shanghai over currency regulations (Singapore newspaper) — consistent but unconfirmed
- 1949: Escaped Shanghai on "the last plane out" during communist revolution
- ~1950: Settled permanently in Sydney
- 1955 (Apr): "Luigi Pirola, Italian businessman from Milan" mentioned in Tokyo newspaper (Singapore NLB) — consistent but unconfirmed
- **1955 (30 Jun): Naturalized as Australian citizen** (Commonwealth Gazette — CONFIRMED)
- 1974: Wife Grace dies (12 Jul)
- 2002: Luigi dies (23 Jan), buried Macquarie Park Cemetery

### Grace Therese Pirola (née Bailor) — Hardy's Great-Grandmother

English. Lived extensively across Europe before meeting Luigi in France. Died 12 Jul 1974, Sydney. Buried Macquarie Park Cemetery.

**The Bailor surname is extremely rare** — only 15 people in all of England carry it. In 1891, 100% of UK Bailors were in **Lancashire** (a strong Catholic region). Her middle name "Therese" (St. Thérèse of Lisieux) suggests Catholic upbringing. Lancashire + Catholic is consistent.

82% of all Bailors worldwide are in Sierra Leone. The name derives from Norman French "baillur/bailleor" (one who delivers goods), related to "bailiff." Warwickshire/Somerset connections in historical records.

### Gino Pirola — Hardy's Grandfather

Son of Luigi and Grace. Died young (~age 40), found dead at a bar — likely heart attack. Married **Haryanti Stuart** (Subud name; birth name unknown). Father of Hanna Thomas (born Anna Pirola).

**Note:** The name "Gino" is confusing because the father Luigi was ALSO known as "Gino." Need clarification on whether Hardy's grandfather was a separate son named Gino, or whether there's a naming overlap.

### Dr. Romano "Ron" Pirola OAM KSG — Hardy's Great-Uncle

Born in Mexico during parents' travels. Anglicized his name from Romano. Studied medicine at University of Sydney, doctorate at UNSW, post-doctoral at Royal Free Hospital London and Mount Sinai New York. Co-founded Pancreatic Research Group at UNSW 1981. 100+ publications.

Married **Mavis** (63+ years). Four children including **Byron Pirola** (b. ~1960, BSc Honours Biochemistry UNSW 1982, partner at EY Port Jackson Partners, married Francine, 5 children).

Honours: Medal of Order of Australia (1997), Papal Knight of St Gregory (1998), Honorary Doctorate ACU (2016), member of the Pontifical Council for the Family (1981–2008). Pope Francis honoured Ron, Mavis, Byron, and Francine at St Mary's Cathedral Sydney, 26 Apr 2019.

### Haryanti Stuart — Hardy's Grandmother

Birth name completely unknown. "Haryanti" is a Subud spiritual name. "Stuart" is from her third husband. Married at least 3 times: (1) Gino Pirola, (2) unknown, (3) Mr. Stuart. Confirmed as a long-time Subud Voice subscriber in Australia. No biographical details beyond that found online.

### The Pirola Surname

Confirmed Lombardy origin — especially the Milan / lower Como / Varese zone. Common in the Metropolitan City of Milan. Cernusco sul Naviglio is an old settlement on the Naviglio Martesana canal corridor, historically tied to irrigation, agriculture, and local trade.
