# Thomas Line — Research Summary

## What We Know

### The Deep Roots (Confirmed via Australian Dictionary of Biography, WikiTree, Geni)

The Thomas family originates from **Ireland**. Rev. Bartholomew Thomas, D.D., was Rector of Cloydagh, Queen's County. His wife was Anna Jocelyn Davidson. Two of their sons emigrated to Tasmania:

**Jocelyn Henry Connor Thomas** (1780–1862)
- Educated at Eton and Trinity College Dublin
- Arrived Van Diemen's Land 2 Feb 1824 on the *William Shand* with wife Charlotte Partridge and 7 children
- Appointed **Colonial Treasurer of Van Diemen's Land** (1825–1832)
- Settled at North Down, near Port Sorell (north of Launceston)
- Had 40+ grandchildren by the time of his death

**Captain Bartholomew Boyle Thomas** (~1785–1831)
- Jocelyn's brother, arrived 1826 on the *Albion*
- Founded the Cressy Company, established North Down property
- **Killed by Aboriginal warriors** during the Black War, 31 Aug 1831
- Married Louisa 1811 but had no children

### Jocelyn's Children (Generation 2)

1. **Bartholomew "Bat" Thomas** — established "Appledore" estate (the apple farm) west of the River Mersey, 1854
2. **Samuel Henry Leipsig Thomas** (1813–1883) — married **Barbara Moore** (1820–1861, died Dublin)
3. **Edward Rice Thomas** — untraced
4. **Charles Stanhope Thomas** — untraced
5. **Henry Cramer Thomas** — untraced
6. **Charlotte Mary Thomas** (1823–1874)
7. At least 2 more daughters

### The Known Tasmanian Branch (Samuel → Turton → Jocelyn Langslow)

Samuel's confirmed son **Turton Samuel Thomas** (1846–1914) stayed in Tasmania. Turton's son **Jocelyn Langslow Thomas** (1886–1961) farmed at "New Ground" and died at Appledore House, Devonport. He had 8 children who mostly remained in Tasmania.

### The Gap

Somewhere between the 1850s and early 1900s, a Thomas branch moved from Tasmania to **Yass, NSW**. Three brothers grew up in Yass:

- **Patrick Moor(e) Thomas** — moved to Wollongong, ran an engineering business supplying BHP
- **Ken Thomas** — details unknown
- **Frank Thomas** — became a Qantas pilot

### Patrick's Family

Patrick married **Jean** (maiden name unknown). They had 5 children:
- **Gregory** (born Phillip, Hardy's father) — attended Scots College, Sydney
- **Michael** — attended Scots College, Sydney
- **Peter** — died young (heroin overdose, incarcerated in Wollongong/Sydney area)
- **Cate**
- **Robin**

### Captain Frank Thomas (d. 23 March 1946)

Frank Thomas commanded Qantas Avro Lancastrian G-AGLX which disappeared over the Indian Ocean on the Negombo (Ceylon) to Cocos Islands leg. 10 on board, no wreckage ever found. Described as "highly experienced." All crew from NSW. Exactly 80 years ago today (as of 23 March 2026).

### Notable Thomas Relatives

- **Daniel Thomas AM** (b. 1931) — inaugural Curator of Australian Art, National Gallery
- **Archbishop Sir Marcus Lawrence Loane KBE** (1911–2009) — grandmother was a Thomas from Port Sorell
- **"Snow Thomas"** — organized the Thomas Tasmanian Bicentennial 2024 at Northdown
- The **Thomas Burial Ground** at Northdown has 35 memorials on private land
- **North Down** property remains in Thomas family ownership to this day

## The Key Clue

Samuel Henry Leipsig Thomas married **Barbara Moore**. Patrick's middle name is **Moor(e)**. This strongly suggests Patrick's line descends from Samuel, with the Moore maiden name preserved as a middle name — a very common naming convention in Australian families.

## What We Don't Know

1. **Patrick's parents** — who was his father? Which generation connects Samuel (1813) to Patrick (~1900s)?
2. **When and why** the family moved from Tasmania to Yass
3. **Jean's maiden name**
4. **Frank's full initials** — "O.F.Y. Thomas" appears in one source but is unconfirmed
5. **Ken Thomas** — is he Kenneth William Thomas (1913–1997), founder of TNT freight? Probably not (different family background), but unconfirmed
6. **Birth and death dates** for Patrick, Ken, Jean
7. **Edward Rice, Charles Stanhope, and Henry Cramer Thomas** — three of Jocelyn's sons are completely untraced. One of these lines may have moved to Yass.
