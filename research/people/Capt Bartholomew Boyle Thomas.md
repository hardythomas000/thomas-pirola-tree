# Captain Bartholomew Boyle Thomas

## Notes

Arrived Tasmania 1826 on *Albion*. Founded Cressy Company, established North Down near Port Sorell. **Killed by Aboriginal warriors** during the Black War. No children. Referenced in *The Black War* by Nicholas Clements.

## Detailed Research (compiled Mar 2026)

### Family Background
- Father: Rev. Bartholomew Thomas, D.D., rector of Cloydagh, squire of Everton, Queen's County (Co. Laois), Ireland
- Mother: Anna Jocelyn (née Davidson)
- Brother: Jocelyn Henry Connor Thomas (1780-1862)
- Sister: Marianne Burrell (youngest; died young)
- Educated at Eton and Trinity College, Dublin

### Military Career
- **13 June 1805**: Cornet, 17th Light Dragoons
- **26 May 1809**: Lieutenant, 9th Light Dragoons
- Later served in 100th Regiment, 4th Garrison Battalion, and 18th Regiment
- **1814**: Resigned by sale of commission
- Post-1814: **Served under Bolivar in South America** (c.1817-1824, specific unit unknown)
  - Context: After Napoleonic Wars, thousands of British/Irish officers volunteered for Bolivar's independence campaigns. An "Irish Legion" of ~1,700 men was formed; over 7,000 British veterans served in the "British Legions"

### Marriage
- Married **Louisa** (maiden name unknown) in 1811
- No children
- When youngest sister Marianne Burrell died, Louisa raised her four orphaned children
- Louisa survived him; died at Stoke Damerel, Devon, England, 12 April 1862

### Arrival and the Cressy Company
- Arrived Hobart on the *Albion*, **3 May 1826**, bringing men, livestock, machinery and stores
- Brought Australia's **first Hereford cattle**, plus Cleveland and Flemish horses, Shorthorn cattle, Merino/Leicester/Southdown sheep
- More than half the horses and many cattle/sheep died on the voyage
- Founded the **New South Wales and Van Diemen's Land Establishment** (later "Cressy Company" or "Horse Breeding Co.") — partnership of 6+ members, initial capital **£24,000**
- Named the property **Cressy** after the Battle of Crécy (1346), where an ancestor was created Knight Banneret
- Secured grant of 10,000 acres + 10,000 acres reserved on west side of Lake River

### The Wreck of the Sally
- Arranged for establishment under **Henry Widowson** to sail to Ringarooma in schooner *Sally*
- *Sally* wrecked near destination on night of **30 June 1826**
- **13 lives lost** including T. Johnson, J. Whatham, Elizabeth Willett and 5 children, J. Brown, Sarah Pilcher, Sarah Hooker
- Much machinery and stores destroyed — **not insured**
- Ringarooma location abandoned; company relocated to Lake River

### The Cressy Company Report (Aug 1827)
- Report to Colonial Secretary John Burnett showed "impressive achievement in developing the holding"
- But financial matters neglected; Sheriff took action
- Home directors dissolved partnership, press notice 28 June 1828
- Lt Thomas Dutton RN succeeded as manager
- Company eventually returned "good capital gain to shareholders in the 1850s"

### North Down and Aboriginal Relations
- Became **"first settler in Devon"** — took over 2,200 acres west of Port Sorell with financial help from Jocelyn
- In three years achieved **notable success establishing friendly relations** with local Aboriginal tribes, while across most of the island there were frequent killings

### Death — 31 August 1831
- **Speared to death** along with overseer **James Parker** by a hostile tribe from the south (not the local tribes he had befriended)
- The attack was led by **Montpelliatta** (c.1790-1836), a Big River clan leader and key organiser of Aboriginal resistance
- Montpelliatta had led a band northward out of the highlands, conducting raids culminating in the killing of Thomas and Parker
- This was one of the "worst and last atrocities" of the Black War
- In December 1831, Montpelliatta surrendered to George Augustus Robinson; exiled to Flinders Island where he died 1836

### Character (from ADB)
- "Imaginative, impulsive and altruistic"
- "Generous and likeable in many ways"
- "Uncommonly active" physically
- Both brothers showed "unwise disregard for risk"

### Legacy
- Jocelyn inherited North Down; his sons later repurchased it after his financial ruin
- North Down "still remains in the hands of Jocelyn's descendants"
- Private Thomas burial ground exists at Northdown (off Northdown Lane)
- ADB entry shared with Jocelyn, written by **H.R. Thomas** (family descendant)
