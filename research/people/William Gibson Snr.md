## Notes

Born **Kilmaurs, Scotland**. Married Mary McAllister at Kilmarnock 1838. 4 sons, 3 daughters.

Arrived Brisbane July 1863 with eldest son Angus on *Cairngorm*. Sugar pioneers: **Clydesdale Mill** at Hemmant 1868 → **Bingera Plantation** 1883 (1,318 acres, Bundaberg). Used **Kanaka labour** — noted for providing some of the worst conditions.

Died age 89 at Bingera, December 1902.

## Detailed Research (compiled Mar 2026)

### Arrival in Queensland
- William Gibson and eldest son **Angus** sailed as assisted immigrants on the **Cairngorm**
- Departed Liverpool 22 March 1863, Greenock 27/28 March 1863
- Arrived **Brisbane (Moreton Bay) 2 July 1863** (~92 days passage)
- Wife **Mary (née MacAlister)** and **five children** followed on the **Warren Hastings**, arriving Brisbane 20 June 1864
- Initially settled at **Doughboy Creek** (later Hemmant) — market garden

### Clydesdale Mill (1866-1883)
- **1866**: Obtained sugar cane cuttings from **Louis Hope** at Ormiston (Hope erected Queensland's first crushing mill in 1864)
- **1868**: Established **Clydesdale Sugar Mill** at Hemmant
- **1869**: Horse-mill installed; **1871**: steam mill purchased — 7 sugar mills operating in Hemmant area by then
- **1880**: New machinery imported from Clydesdale, Glasgow; mill processed cane from ~230 acres
- Late 1870s: Drought and disease destroyed crops, making Brisbane-area sugar unviable
- **1883/84**: Clydesdale Mill sold and closed; family relocated to Bundaberg
- Heritage: "Dumbarton" (Gibson House) at Hemmant, QLD Heritage Register ID 602382

### Bingera Plantation (1883-2020)
- **1883**: William purchased land north of Burnett River near Bundaberg; partnership with **John Howes and Bros** (also from Hemmant)
- **1885**: Large crushing mill operational — first crushing
- **By 1889**: Capital £73,000; 2,658 acres; **98 Europeans, 8 Chinese, 200 Kanakas** employed
- Became **biggest mill in the Bundaberg area**; undivided plantation structure
- Gibson & Howes built railway siding; "Gibsons Siding" renamed **Bingera 1897**
- **1894 interview** (Brisbane Courier): nearly 6,000 acres total; mill capacity 50-55 tons daily; 150 Kanakas, 100+ whites during crushing
- **1904**: Partnership floated as **Gibson & Howes Limited**
- **1972**: Merged with Fairymead Sugar to form **Bundaberg Sugar Company Limited**
- **2020**: Bingera Mill **permanently closed** after 135 years — low cane supply

### Kanaka (South Sea Islander) Labour
- Between 1863-1904: ~62,000 South Sea Islanders brought to Queensland sugar industry
- By 1889: Bingera employed **200 Kanakas**; by 1894: **150 Kanakas**
- Angus Gibson quoted: *"I have seen no one to equal the kanaka in outdoor work"*
- Death rates appalling — average 50 per 1,000 per year; worst year 1884: **147 per 1,000**
- **Between Dec 1895 and Dec 1900**: 135 Islander deaths at Bingera alone
- Workers buried on plantations; 600+ Islanders in Bundaberg general cemetery
- **1901**: Pacific Island Labourers Act (White Australia Policy) ended the trade

### Son Angus Gibson (1842-1920)
- Born **25 April 1842**, Kilmaurs, Ayrshire, Scotland
- Married **Catherine Martin**, 9 November 1866 — five daughters, three sons
- Managing partner of Bingera from 1882/83
- Member, Queensland Legislative Council (appointed 6 April 1899; served until death)
- Chairman, Bundaberg Harbor Board; Chairman, Toowoomba Electric Lighting Co.
- Prominent Methodist layman
- Died **28 May 1920**, South Brisbane; buried South Kolan Cemetery
- ADB entry: https://adb.anu.edu.au/biography/gibson-angus-3607

### Connection to Thomas Family
The Gibson family connects to the Thomas line through **Jean MacAlister Gibson** (1922-2000), granddaughter of Angus Alexander Gibson (son of Angus Gibson above), who married **Patrick Moore Thomas** — son of Owen Thomas and Phyllis Youl, brother of Capt Owen Frank Youl Thomas.
