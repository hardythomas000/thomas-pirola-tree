## Notes

Educated at Eton and Trinity College Dublin. Arrived Tasmania 2 Feb 1824 on *William Shand*. Colonial Treasurer of Van Diemen's Land 1825–1832. Settled at North Down, Port Sorell. Had 40+ grandchildren at death.

## Detailed Research (compiled Mar 2026)

### Family Background
- Father: Rev. Bartholomew Thomas, D.D., rector of Cloydagh, squire of Everton, Queen's County (Co. Laois), Ireland
- Mother: Anna Jocelyn (née Davidson)
- Brother: Bartholomew Boyle Thomas (c.1785-1831)
- Sister: Marianne Burrell (youngest)
- Educated at Eton and Trinity College, Dublin

### Marriage and Children
- **Married 11 April 1808** to Charlotte, daughter of Henry Partridge, chief justice of Ely
- **Nine children total**: 7 born before emigration, 2 more daughters born at Roseway Lodge, Hobart
- Known children: Bartholomew William ("Bat" Sr), "Bat" (second son, pioneered Mersey district 1854), Charlotte Mary, Samuel Henry Leipsig, Edward Rice, Charles Stanhope, Henry Cramer
- By death: **40+ grandchildren**
- Charlotte died 13 November 1873

### Ireland — Before Emigration
- Agent for estates of Lord Stanhope and Lady Portarlington
- 1813: Leased "entire slobs in the harbour of Wexford" to reclaim 1,100 acres from the sea
- "An unprecedented high tide destroyed in one hour the labour of more than seven years" — **£40,000 loss**
- Short time in France, then emigrated

### Emigration and Arrival
- Arrived Van Diemen's Land **2 February 1824** on the *William Shand* (ADB says "in the Derwent" — the river/port)
- With wife Charlotte, 7 children, 3 servants and their 2 children
- **Thomas Tasmanian Bicentennial 2024** celebrated 200th anniversary of this arrival (2-4 Feb 2024, Northdown)

### Properties in Tasmania
1. **Initial grant**: 1,000 acres on South Esk south of Snake Banks (Powranna) — rejected as flood-prone
2. **Everton**: ~500 acres near Evandale, acquired from Donald Campbell (named after Irish family estate)
3. **Milford**: 500 acres on South Esk at Conara — granted to son Bartholomew William
4. **Additional blocks** east of Milford: totalled ~8,000 acres (all sold 1833)
5. **Roseway Lodge**: 10 acres on New Town Rivulet, 3 miles from Hobart Town (town residence)
6. **North Down**: Inherited from brother Bartholomew after his death 1831

### Colonial Treasurer (1825-1832)
- Appointed acting Colonial Treasurer by Lt-Gov Sir George Arthur ~Oct 1824
- Confirmed **4 July 1825**
- Full title: Colonial Treasurer, Collector of Internal Revenue, Inspector of Breweries and Distilleries
- Member of Legislative Council and Justice of the Peace
- One of **five members** of the first Executive Council when VDL became independent from NSW
- Increased customs collections, earning Arthur's praise but displeasing merchants

### The Treasury Scandal
- Late 1820s: borrowed too deeply to buy land
- **23 October 1832**: Snap audit found serious shortage in Treasury chest
- Initial estimate: **£7,112 6s. 1d.**
- Official finding (1834): **£10,627 10s. 5¼d.**
- Jocelyn disputed the figure; admitted shortage but not misappropriation
- Claimed frequent unreported robberies — "not accepted as a valid explanation"
- Evidence of **collusion with Rolla O'Ferrall** (customs collector) emerged
- Both dismissed from office; O'Ferrall later committed fraud forging deeds
- John West: "Capital had been borrowed from the chest without authority... The money was, however, restored"

### Court Proceedings — Thomas, In re [1833] TASSupC 38
- Supreme Court of VDL, 29 November 1833
- Mr. Gellibrand moved for rule requiring Crown Solicitor Ross to repay £4,000 to O'Ferrall
- O'Ferrall had paid £4,000 on Thomas's Crown debt, claiming he was misled about the total amount
- Both judges refused the motion
- Jocelyn's estate "sold under extent at the suit of the Crown and executions of other creditors"
- Source: https://www7.austlii.edu.au/cgi-bin/viewdoc/au/other/TASSupC/1833/38.html

### After the Scandal
- All properties sold to satisfy creditors and Crown
- Retired to son's property **Milford**, later to **North Down** (which sons managed to buy back)
- Persistent but vain efforts to have circumstances re-examined judicially
- Expected inquiry would "give me back my reputation"
- Lord Stanhope had declared: "His character is irreproachable"

### Character (from ADB)
- Henry Savery described: "a hurried quickness in his manner, a sort of absentism... a little of the brogue"
- Granddaughter remembered him walking "very erect" in garden at North Down
- "In Ireland he had been 'universally beloved'"
- Passion for buying land "probably based on a genuine love of the land"

### Death
- Died **7 April 1862** at North Down, aged ~82
- Charlotte died **13 November 1873**

### Legacy
- ADB entry written by **H.R. Thomas** (family descendant), shared with brother Bartholomew
- Notable descendants include Daniel Thomas (inaugural Curator of Australian Art, NGA 1978) and Archbishop Sir Marcus Loane
- Thomas Burial Ground at Northdown (off Northdown Lane)
