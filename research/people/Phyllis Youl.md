## Research (compiled Mar 2026)

### The Youl Family of Perth, Tasmania

Phyllis likely belongs to the Youl family of Perth, Tasmania — one of the most prominent pastoral families in Tasmanian history. This is confirmed by her son's name: **Captain Owen Frank Youl Thomas** (the middle name "Youl" being the maternal surname).

The family descends from **John Youl**, who came to Tasmania as a missionary from Tahiti. His son **Sir James Arndell Youl KCMG** (1811-1904) inherited **Symmons Plains** near Perth, Tasmania, and became famous for introducing brown trout to Australia. Sir James had five sons and eight daughters with his wife Eliza (née Cox).

One of Sir James's sons, **Alfred Youl MLA, MLC** (1849-1921), was a farmer and politician at Symmons Plains. He had "three sons and four daughters" across two marriages — Phyllis may be among the unnamed daughters, or from another branch of the Youl family.

**Notable Youl family members**:
- Sir James Arndell Youl KCMG — pastoralist, introduced trout to Australia (ADB entry: https://adb.anu.edu.au/biography/youl-sir-james-arndell-4899)
- Alfred Youl MLA, MLC — farmer, politician (ADB entry: https://adb.anu.edu.au/biography/youl-alfred-9212)
- Geoffrey Arthur Douglas Youl MC — WWI Military Cross, WWII commander at Darwin
- John Beresford Osmond Youl — killed at Bullecourt, France, 1917
