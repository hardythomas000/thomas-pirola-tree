# Message for Mum & Dad

---

Hi Mum and Dad,

I've been doing some family history research and I've managed to trace quite a lot already, but I've hit some gaps that only you'd be able to help with. I'd love it if you could have a think and let me know anything you remember about the following.

## Dad's Side (Thomas)

I've traced the Thomas family back to **Jocelyn Henry Connor Thomas**, who arrived in Tasmania in 1824 from Ireland. He was Colonial Treasurer of Van Diemen's Land. His brother Captain Bartholomew was killed during the Black War in 1831. The family had the "Appledore" apple farm near the Mersey River, and the North Down property near Port Sorell is still in the family.

I also found that one of Jocelyn's sons, **Samuel Henry Leipsig Thomas**, married a woman named **Barbara Moore** — which I think might explain the "Moore/Moor" middle name in Grandpa Patrick's name.

What I'm missing is **the generations between the 1850s Tasmania Thomases and Grandpa Patrick growing up in Yass**. Can you help with any of this?

- **Who were Patrick's parents?** (Your grandparents, Dad.) What were their names? Were they from Tasmania originally, or had the family already moved to Yass by then?
- **Do you know roughly when Patrick was born?** Even a decade would help.
- **What was Grandma Jean's maiden name?**
- **Uncle Frank** — the Qantas pilot who died in the plane crash in 1946. Do you know his full name? One source calls him "Captain O.F.Y. Thomas" — does that ring a bell? What do the initials stand for?
- **Uncle Ken** — what do you know about him? There's a Kenneth William Thomas from Harden (near Yass) who founded TNT freight — any chance that's the same Ken?
- **Do you remember the name of Patrick's engineering business in Wollongong?**
- **Do you know roughly when Peter died?** (Even the decade would help for records searches.)
- **Cate and Robin** — do you know their married names or where they ended up?
- **Does the name "Snow Thomas" mean anything to you?** They organised a Thomas family bicentennial reunion at Northdown, Tasmania in 2024.

## Mum's Side (Pirola / Stuart)

I've confirmed that **Great-Grandpa Luigi "Gino" Pirola** was born in 1905 in Cernusco sul Naviglio near Milan, and was naturalised Australian in 1955. I found newspaper mentions of a Luigi Pirola in Shanghai in 1948 and Tokyo in 1955, which fits the stories about his travels. Uncle Ron was born in Mexico while they were travelling.

**Great-Grandma Grace Therese Bailor** — she was English, but "Bailor" is an incredibly rare surname (only about 15 people in all of England have it). The few historical records point to Lancashire, which was a strongly Catholic area. She met Luigi in France.

Here's what I'm hoping you might know:

- **Grandpa Gino** (your dad, Mum) — do you know his actual birth date and where he was born? Was he born in Australia or overseas?
- **Do you know Grace's parents' names?** Or where in England she was from?
- **Who is Luigi Ignatius Pirola?** He's buried at the same cemetery as Luigi and Grace (Macquarie Park). Was he Luigi's brother? Father?
- **Antonio Osvaldo Ettore Pirola** (1930–2014, buried at Rookwood) — was he one of Luigi and Grace's sons?
- **Mum — what was Grandma Haryanti's name before Subud?** Her birth name / maiden name is the biggest gap in the whole family tree. Even a first name or surname before her marriages would open up a lot.
- **Do you know who Haryanti's second husband was?** (Before Mr. Stuart.)

## Any Documents?

If either of you have any of the following tucked away, they'd be incredibly useful:

- Old photos with names/dates written on the back
- Birth, death, or marriage certificates (especially for Patrick, Jean, Gino, or Grace)
- Letters, Christmas cards, or funeral notices
- Any family tree that someone put together at some point
- The "Christmas blessings to all" PDF that came through — is that from the Pirola family?

No rush on any of this. Even small details or half-remembered things are useful — I can cross-reference anything you give me against the records.
