# Phase Planning

How to plan genealogy research in phases, from free and immediate to expensive and long-term.

## The Four Phases

### Phase 1: Immediate (Free, Existing Data)
**Goal**: Extract maximum value from data already in hand and free online sources.

**Actions**:
- Organize and OCR all physical documents
- Fill in Family_Tree.md with everything you know
- Run the tree expansion prompt (searches free databases)
- Run the cross-reference audit
- Run the Find a Grave sweep
- Search FamilySearch wiki pages (free, no login for wiki)
- Search free databases: Digitalarkivet (Norway), Geneteka (Poland), Matricula (Catholic records)
- Build the master timeline
- Create person files for key ancestors

**Cost**: $0
**Time**: 1 to 3 sessions with AI assistance
**Expected yield**: 20 to 60 new ancestors, organized vault, identified gaps

### Phase 2: Web Research (Free, Targeted Searches)
**Goal**: Systematically search every free online database for records that would resolve open questions.

**Actions**:
- Search emigration/immigration databases
- Search census records (FamilySearch)
- Search newspaper archives (Chronicling America, state digital hubs)
- Search county/parish-specific databases
- Attempt to access restricted databases through free trials
- Log every search (positive and negative)

**Cost**: $0 (or free trial subscriptions)
**Time**: 1 to 3 sessions
**Expected yield**: Filling gaps in existing lines, resolving 2 to 5 open questions

### Phase 3: Paid Databases and Written Requests
**Goal**: Access records behind paywalls and request records from archives.

**Actions**:
- Subscribe to Ancestry.com (monthly) for targeted searches
- Subscribe to Newspapers.com for obituaries and biographical articles
- Order vital records from county clerks ($10 to $30 each)
- Submit written requests to foreign archives (IKG Wien, state archives)
- Upload DNA to GEDmatch ($0) or FamilyTreeDNA ($19 transfer)
- Request USCIS naturalization records ($65 each)

**Cost**: $50 to $300 depending on how many records you need
**Time**: 2 to 8 weeks (waiting for responses)
**Expected yield**: Resolving stubborn questions, extending lines into foreign records

### Phase 4: Long Horizon
**Goal**: Pursue leads that require significant investment of time or money.

**Actions**:
- Conduct oral history interviews with elderly relatives
- Visit archives in person (courthouses, national archives, foreign archives)
- Hire a professional genealogist for specific research problems
- Obtain physical copies of local history books
- Test additional family members' DNA for phasing
- Maintain saved searches and alerts on genealogy platforms
- Build durable research infrastructure (hypothesis tracking, citation standards)

**Cost**: $100+ depending on scope
**Time**: Ongoing
**Expected yield**: Breaking through brick walls, confirming or refuting hypotheses

## Planning Template

For each research phase, define:

1. **Objective**: What you aim to accomplish
2. **Tasks**: Numbered list of specific actions
3. **Deliverables**: What files will be created or updated
4. **Dependencies**: What must be done first
5. **Done when**: How you will know the phase is complete
6. **Budget**: Expected costs (if any)
