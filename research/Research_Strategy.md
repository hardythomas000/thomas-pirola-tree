# Research Strategy

## Executive Assessment

### What Is Built
- Initial vault structure with templates
- Skeleton family tree with Hardy Thomas as root person
- Two family lines identified: Thomas (Australian) and wife's Indonesian family
- No documentary sources yet

### What Is Credible
- Hardy's own biographical data (Strong Signal)
- Children's names (Strong Signal)
- Wife's Indonesian background (Strong Signal)
- Everything else: unknown / not yet researched

### Where the Biggest Leverage Lies
1. **Hardy providing parent/grandparent names** — unlocks all downstream research
2. **Australian BMD registries** — births, deaths, marriages are well-digitized
3. **Find a Grave / cemetery records** — free, often has photos of headstones
4. **FamilySearch** — free, extensive Australian records
5. **Trove (National Library of Australia)** — newspaper archives, free
6. **Indonesian civil records** — more challenging, may need family oral history

## Research Plan

### Phase 1: Immediate (Data Collection from Hardy)
**Objective**: Populate the tree with known family members
**Tasks**:
1. Get parents' full names, birth dates/locations
2. Get grandparents' names (both sides)
3. Get wife's family names (parents, grandparents)
4. Any known family stories, migration history, places of origin
5. Scan any existing documents (certificates, photos, letters)
**Deliverables**: Updated Family_Tree.md with at least 2 generations
**Done when**: All known family members are documented

### Phase 2: Web Research (Free Sources)
**Objective**: Extend tree using free genealogical databases
**Tasks**:
1. Run autoresearch prompt 01 (tree expansion) on Thomas line
2. Search Trove for newspaper mentions (obituaries, marriage notices)
3. Search Find a Grave for Thomas family burials
4. Search FamilySearch for Australian BMD records
5. Search WikiTree / Geni for existing Thomas family trees
**Dependencies**: Phase 1 complete (need names to search)
**Done when**: Each known ancestor has been searched across all free sources

### Phase 3: External Data and Human Action
**Objective**: Access paid databases and physical archives
**Tasks**:
1. Check QLD BDM registry (births, deaths, marriages)
2. Check VIC BDM registry (Melbourne connection)
3. Consider Ancestry.com subscription for census records
4. Contact Indonesian family for oral history and documents
**Dependencies**: Phase 2 findings to guide targeted searches

### Phase 4: Long Horizon
**Objective**: Build durable research infrastructure
**Tasks**:
1. Generate GEDCOM file for tree software compatibility
2. Create person files for each documented ancestor
3. Cross-reference and audit all sources
4. Resolve data discrepancies

## Web and Archival Research Agenda

### Australia
**Search targets**: Thomas family — parents, grandparents, great-grandparents
**Likely sources**:
- QLD BDM (bdm.qld.gov.au)
- VIC BDM (bdm.vic.gov.au) — Melbourne years
- Trove (trove.nla.gov.au) — newspaper archives
- FamilySearch (familysearch.org) — Australian collections
- Find a Grave (findagrave.com)
- WikiTree, Geni
**Hypotheses being tested**: Thomas surname origin (English? Welsh? Other?)
**Decisive records**: Birth/marriage certificates of Hardy's grandparents

### Indonesia
**Search targets**: Wife's family — parents, grandparents
**Likely sources**:
- Indonesian civil registration (limited online)
- Dutch colonial records (for pre-1945 — available via FamilySearch)
- Family oral history (primary source)
- Local mosque/church records
**Hypotheses being tested**: Geographic origin within Indonesia, ethnic background
**Decisive records**: Marriage certificate, family register (kartu keluarga)
