## Subud Context

Some of both the Thomas and Pirola families are members of Subud. Members receive spiritual names through the latihan:
- Gregory Thomas was born **Phillip Thomas**, received Subud name **Faisal**, later took **Gregory**
- Hanna Thomas was born **Anna Pirola**, received Subud name **Halimah**, later took **Hanna**
- Roma Spalding received Subud name **Haryanti**, later married Mr. Stuart
- Hardy's wife Noor Julaiha is Indonesian but **not** Subud

**For research**: Legal/civil names (Phillip Thomas, Anna Pirola, Roma Spalding) are what appear in government records.

### Generation 1 — Tasmania (1820s)

**Jocelyn Henry Connor Thomas** (b. 1780; d. 7 Apr 1862, Port Sorell)
- Arrived 2 Feb 1824 on ***Derwent*** (NOT William Shand as previously recorded) with wife, 7 children, 3 servants
- Educated at Eton and Trinity College Dublin

- In Ireland: agent for estates of Lord Stanhope and Lady Portarlington. Leased Wexford Harbour slobs to reclaim 1,100 acres — "an unprecedented high tide destroyed in one hour the labour of more than seven years" (loss £40,000). Emigrated while poor.

- **TREASURY SCANDAL**: snap audit 23 Oct 1832 found shortage of **£10,627**. Admitted shortage but denied misappropriation — claimed frequent unreported robberies. Not accepted. Dismissed. All properties sold. Collector O'Ferrall also dismissed (later convicted of forging deeds).

## PIROLA LINE (Maternal)

*Source: "The Australian Pirola Family" — Ron Pirola, Family Reunion 3-4 June 2023, Melbourne (18 pages)*

### Italian Origins — Sondrio, Lombardy

The Pirola surname originates from **Sondrio**, a town in the northernmost valleys of Italy (near the Swiss border). There is a **Lake Pirola** near Sondrio (~2000m altitude). The surname is concentrated in Lombardy — 1,956 families in Italy, 1,710 in Lombardy.

**Luigi Pirola** (great-great-grandfather) — cheesemaker from Sondrio. Had **17 children**.

**Luigi Pirola** (great-grandfather, 6th of 17 children, also named Luigi)
- Also became a cheesemaker like his father
- Married **Maria Rinaldi** from a neighbouring town
- ~1930: moved to Milan, ran "highly successful grocery business"
- Built family mausoleum in cemetery (still in use)
- Mild-mannered, absent-minded; wife Maria was "very demanding, sharp temper"
- Three children: **Luigi/Gino**, **Paolo**, **Alice**
- Five of his sisters became nuns (one, Matilda, in enclosed Jerusalem convent)

### Luigi "Gino" Pirola (17 Feb 1905–23 Jan 2002) — Hardy's Great-Grandfather

- Born **17 February 1905** (NAA passenger arrival record A12508, 31/4062)
- At 12, left school during WWI (father away fighting Austrians), worked as delivery boy
- Drafted into army after school — laid telephone lines in Alps
- Accomplished boxer (fast reflexes)
- Office job at **Chatillon** (synthetic textile company), did night accountancy course
- Chatillon formed combine **Italviscosa** — 3rd largest synthetic textile manufacturer after Courtalds (UK) and DuPont (USA)
- Innovative, sent abroad to open new markets — continued until retirement at **age 87**
- Nickname at work: **"the lion"** — "I could send this man into the Sahara Desert without a shirt on his back and he would come back with a new market" (chairman's quote)
- Honoured by Italian Government with **"Stella di Lavoro"** (Star of Work) for Italian exports
- Met **Grace Barlow** on business visit to France. She spoke no Italian, he no English — communicated in French
- First in family to have email address; in late 80s mastered microwave cooking in two weeks
- Doing philosophy courses at Sydney University in his early 90s
- After Grace's death, remarried **Patricia Hildebrandt** of Sydney (25 years together)
- **Died of heart attack at transport department** trying to renew driver's licence, just before turning 97
- Buried: Macquarie Park Cemetery, North Ryde, NSW

**Life timeline:**
- 17 Feb 1905: Born (confirmed NAA A12508, 31/4062)
- 1932: Chatillon sent him to **Barcelona** for ~4 years
- 1933: First son Gino Jr born in Barcelona
- ~1936: Left Spain as civil war started
- 1936: Sent to Latin America, base in Mexico City. Ron born September 1936
- 1939: Brief return to Milan HQ. Saw German military, WWII about to start. Arranged "6-month business trip" to Australia (the furthest place he could think of)
- **30 Nov 1939: Arrived Sydney per M/N ESQUILINO** — Luigi, Grace (age 32), Gino Jr (age 6), Romano (age 3). NAA A12508, 31/4062. Ship was Lloyd Triestino liner *Esquilino* (8,657 GRT, built 1925 Trieste, 42 first class + 190 third class). Captured by British in Gulf of Aden 10 Jun 1940 on homeward voyage, renamed *Empire Governor*, scrapped Scotland 1946. Family story says "sunk by Japanese submarine" but actually seized as war prize. Confusion may stem from sister ship *Romolo* which was scuttled off Queensland coast 12 Jun 1940, or *Viminale* torpedoed 1943.
- 1939-1945: Enemy alien in Australia. Rented house Baulkham Hills, then Castle Hill (bred ducks). Got job at Apostolic Nunciature translating POW messages (North Sydney). Avoided internment thanks to English wife. **Gaie and Mark born in Sydney.**
- Post-war: Shanghai for 2 years. As communists advanced, escaped on **last plane out** to Hong Kong (Grace got 6 tickets at last airline she tried). Flew to Europe.
- Gino and Ron sent to **boarding schools in Switzerland**; Grace stayed in Milan with Gaie and Mark
- Father traveled: India, Pakistan, Russia, Japan, South Korea
- **11 Sep 1950: Luigi + Romano arrived Sydney by aircraft G-ALEO** (NAA SP244/2, N1950/2/10120)
- Permanently settled in Australia. House in **Roseville** (now Opus Dei HQ)
- **1955: Naturalized Australian** (Commonwealth Gazette, 30 Jun 1955)
- Grace died peacefully in her sleep, age 62
- Remarried Patricia Hildebrandt
- ~2002: Died age ~97
